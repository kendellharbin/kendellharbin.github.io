Bookology

Fred and I proposed to host a workshop at the PLUG Projects book fair. "In a 2 hour workshop, participants will create a site-specific booklet, learning everything from pre-production to distribution of the final product. Beginning in the gallery with a basic run down of Risograph printing, students will gain an understanding of page layout, printing possibilities, and content development. After a quick dip in the think tank, we’ll surface elsewhere to collect information with which to fill our booklets. Like brave explorers, we will march into the streets and seek out unseen details. This may take the form of poems, doodles, drawings, rubbings, field notes, etc. When our adventure concludes, we’ll retire to the cozy corners of Plug to ask the inevitable: What do we do with what we’ve done? *All participants will leave with an original book of joint creation.”

Business vs Pleasure

A collaboration between myself and my dearest homie Frederick Vorder-Bruegge: a recreation of our office/kitchen space with pertinent details such as our dog (Stanley) creeping out of the cabinet and casual internet perusing via toast disk drive. These are images of the installation at the 2013 KCAI BFA exhibition hosted by the H & R Block Artspace, where it won “best in show”.
